This document records the people whose work has shaped
**BGII:EE Unofficial Item Pack** and its community-maintained repository.

The original mod and the GitHub maintenance project are related, but they are
not the same act of authorship. The distinctions below are intentional.

## Historical community acknowledgements

The original Beamdog discussion contains bug reports, compatibility research,
testing, replacement resources, mirrors, and other assistance from community
members.

Those contributions should be researched and credited carefully rather than
attributed from memory.

A person may be added to this section when:

- the contribution can be linked to a public post or supplied archive;
- the contributor’s preferred public name is known;
- the description accurately distinguishes reporting, testing, implementation,
  translation, or hosting;
- including the credit does not imply that the work was merged into a release
  when it was not.

Suggested format:

| Contributor | Historical contribution | Source |
|---|---|---|
| _Name or handle_ | _What the person reported, supplied, tested, translated, or hosted_ | _Forum post or archive link_ |

## Credit policy

### Significant accepted contributions

A contributor should normally be listed when an accepted contribution includes:

- a code or WeiDU change;
- a new or substantially revised translation;
- repeated or substantial testing;
- an implemented compatibility fix;
- a new item or component design;
- significant documentation;
- release engineering;
- historical reconstruction supported by primary evidence;
- authorized artwork or screenshot preparation.

### Smaller contributions

Small corrections may be credited through:

- Git history;
- the merged pull request;
- release notes;
- a collective acknowledgement.

A contributor may still request inclusion in this document when a seemingly
small correction required meaningful research or specialist knowledge.

### Bug reporters

Reporting a bug and fixing a bug are distinct contributions.

A bug reporter may be credited when the report:

- identifies a previously unknown problem;
- supplies a reliable reproduction;
- provides diagnostic files or compatibility research;
- materially enables a fix.

Credit should not imply that the reporter wrote the eventual patch unless they
did so.

### Translators

Translation credits should identify:

- language;
- locale when relevant;
- translator or translation team;
- proofreading contributors;
- first release containing the translation;
- whether the translation is complete or partial.

### Preferred identity

Contributors may be listed by:

- real name;
- GitHub handle;
- forum handle;
- another stable public name.

Do not publish private names, email addresses, or other personal information
without explicit permission.

## How to add or correct a credit

Open an issue or pull request containing:

1. the preferred display name;
2. an optional public profile link;
3. a concise description of the contribution;
4. the relevant pull request, commit, release, archive, or forum post;
5. any correction needed to an existing attribution.

Attribution disputes should be handled carefully and with documentary evidence.

## Authorship statement

This repository must never describe the community maintainer as the creator of
the original mod.

The preferred wording is:

> BGII:EE Unofficial Item Pack was originally created by Edvin and is now
> preserved and maintained through a community-led continuation coordinated by
> DaveTheBrave.
